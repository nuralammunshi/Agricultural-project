Categorization of Guides

To make your learning experience easier, our guides are categorized based on crop types, seasons, and farming methods.

By Crop Type

By Season

Spring planting involves sowing seeds and transplanting young plants as temperatures rise and soil conditions improve. This critical phase allows farmers to take advantage of longer daylight hours and warmer weather, fostering optimal growth for a variety of crops.

By Farming Method

Conventional farming is an agricultural practice that relies on synthetic fertilizers, pesticides, and herbicides to enhance crop yields and control pests. This method prioritizes high production efficiency and often involves monoculture, where a single crop is grown over large areas.

Organic farming is an eco-friendly agricultural approach that emphasizes natural processes and biodiversity, avoiding synthetic chemicals and genetically modified organisms. It focuses on building healthy soil, promoting sustainable practices, and fostering a balanced ecosystem to enhance crop health and resilience.

Permaculture is a holistic design system that integrates agricultural practices with sustainable living, emphasizing harmony with nature. It aims to create self-sustaining ecosystems by mimicking natural patterns, promoting biodiversity, and using renewable resources.
